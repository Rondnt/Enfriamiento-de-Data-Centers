# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Engineering students and their professors in a Differential Equations course. The student presents this live during an oral evaluation or submits it as a web app assignment. The professor evaluates rigor, correctness, and presentation quality.

## Product Purpose

Visualizes Newton's Law of Cooling applied to real data center server temperature management. Lets users adjust cooling parameters (T₀, Tamb, k) via sliders and immediately compare the analytical solution against Euler and Runge-Kutta 4 numerical methods. Manages a live database of real DC servers and their temperature readings. Demonstrates both the math and the real-world application in one tool.

## Brand Personality

Technical, Scientific, Precise. Feels like a physics instrumentation dashboard — confident in the engineering, never decorative for decoration's sake.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap blue-button templates
- Flat PowerPoint / Excel-style dashboards
- SaaS marketing landing pages
- Empty minimalism with no visual personality

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the protagonist** — every design decision makes the charts and numerical results easier to read and trust.
2. **Engineering confidence** — the UI communicates precision through consistent typographic scales, monospace numerics, and deliberate visual weight.
3. **Thematic coherence** — the visual language connects to the subject matter: cooling, thermal systems, temperature ranges translate into the color strategy.
4. **Dense but breathable** — the tool packs a lot of information; spacing and hierarchy must guide the eye without reducing density.
5. **Academic credibility** — looks rigorous enough that a professor takes it seriously, distinctive enough that it stands apart from template work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Dark theme (appropriate for the engineering/scientific register). All text must meet 4.5:1 contrast. Critical threshold alerts must not rely on color alone.
